Experience

Life Cycle Analyst

Department of Chemical Engineering | University of Calgary

This research internship took place under the supervision of Dr. Joule Bergerson sponsored by Alberta Innovates. The project investigated energy storage and CO2 conversion to fuels using novel nanomaterials. My focus was to perform a Life Cycle Assessment using ISO 14044 of catalyst LCFCr which was developed at University of Calgary.

For context, LCFCr is an electrocatalyst used in SOFC (solid-oxide fuel cells) that facilitates the conversion of CO2 to syngas (and syngas to CO2 as it is a reversible catalyst). Currently the LCA studies published on SOFC fuel cells did not include catalyst extraction and synthesis emissions in their assessment. It is important to have a robust analysis of catalysts used in fuel cells as they require rare earth metals and a very energy intensive synthesis. An excel model was developed for the LCA. A sensitivity analysis was also carried out for factors such as batch size and grid emission intensity. Similar methodology was also applied on other catalysts such as BSFAl, Ni-LSCeCrF, and NSCF and compared from an environmental and cost perspective.

A secondary objective of this research was to simulate and scale up the synthesis of the catalyst to better understand commercialization impact. In order to understand the synthesis, I carried out extensive literature search, using scientific articles and patents. The simulation and scale-up was performed on AspenPlus. I communicated findings, challenges, recommendations, and alternative emission estimation and synthesis techniques in a formal report and presentation to Dr. Joule.

May 2020 - Jan 2021

Research Assistant

Department of Mechanical Engineering | University of Calgary

Working with Dr. Budiman on the “Self-Balancing Unicycle” was a fascinating experience. It was the first time I witnessed a professor in his element trying to do something no one has ever done before. After 3-years of math, Dr. Budiman came up with a series of dynamic equations that predicted the motion of a single-wheeled robot. So far only linearized kinematic models have been developed. He further explains it in his paper “Stability Control Mechanisms and Stable Rectilinear Solution of Unicycle Motion” which I do not understand a word of. My objective was to prove his equations worked. And the best way to do that is to develop a prototype. After several discussions I started working modelling it on SolidWorks. I created drawings and simulations and we went back-and forth for a while till we decided to manufacture it. The best way to manufacture a prototype these days is to 3D print it. Simultaneous I carried out equipment sizing and selection and programmed the Arduino based motor control system.

The 1st iteration was not successful. But Dr. Budiman was very happy that we moved several steps forward in making his dream come true. Below is a picture of the engineering drawing and a build model.
